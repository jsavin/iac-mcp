## Executive Summary

**Recommendation:** Aggressive premium positioning with freemium model
- **Free tier:** Open source MCP bridge (developer adoption, validation)
- **Pro tier:** $19.99/month or $149/year (native app, full features)
- **Lifetime:** $299 limited early adopter (cash flow, champions)
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing (future, 18+ months out)

**Rationale:**
1. Market timing supports premium pricing (AI agentic space is hot)
2. Novel technology (JITD) justifies premium vs basic competitors
3. Freemium drives adoption, premium captures value
4. Bootstrap needs revenue velocity, not just user count
5. Can always reduce price, can't easily raise it

**Revenue Target:** $100K ARR in 18 months (1,000 Pro users @ $100 avg or 500 @ $200)

## Rationale for $19.99/month

### 1. Market Positioning

**Premium tier without ultra-premium risk:**
- Comparable to: Claude Max ($20), Cursor ($20), ChatGPT Plus ($20)
- Above: Basic automation tools ($10-15)
- Below: Ultra-premium ($30+)
- **Signal:** "Serious AI-powered tool, not a hobby project"

**JITD justifies premium:**
- Novel technology (no one else has it)
- Saves hours per week (demonstrable ROI)
- Works with 100+ apps (broader than competitors)
- AI-powered (commands premium)

### 2. Revenue Math

**Path to $100K ARR:**
- 550 Pro users @ $180/year average = $99K ARR
- OR: 300 annual @ $149 + 150 monthly @ $240 = $100K ARR
- OR: 200 lifetime @ $299 + 350 Pro @ $180 = $122K ARR

**Why this works for bootstrap:**
- Hit $100K with ~500-600 paying users
- At 3% conversion: need 18,000 free users (achievable)
- At 5% conversion: need 11,000 free users (conservative)
- Open source + good product â†’ 10-20K free users in 18 months is realistic

**CAC economics:**
- Revenue per user: $180/year
- Target CAC: $30-50 (paid ads, if needed)
- LTV: $400-600 (24-30 month retention)
- LTV:CAC ratio: 8-12:1 (healthy)
- Mostly organic (free tier, content, community) â†’ lower CAC

### 3. Customer Psychology

**$19.99 is a "sweet spot":**
- Not impulse ($9.99) but not considered ($50)
- "One streaming service" mental anchor
- "Less than one lunch/week" justification
- High enough to signal quality
- Low enough to trial without approval (personal credit card)

**Anchoring strategy:**
- Lifetime at $299 makes $19.99 seem reasonable
- Annual at $149 encourages commitment (saves $90)
- Monthly flexibility for cautious buyers

**Justification framework:**
```
$19.99/month = $0.66/day

If it saves 1 hour/week:
- Your time worth $20/hr â†’ $80/month value
- ROI: 4:1 (pay $20, save $80 in time)

If you're a freelancer/consultant:
- Your rate $100/hr â†’ 1 saved hour = pays for 5 months
```

### 4. Competitive Positioning

**vs Zapier ($20-50/month):**
- **Us:** Native apps, local processing, JITD
- **Them:** Web services, pre-built integrations
- **Position:** "For native apps what Zapier is for web services"
- **Pricing:** Same tier ($20) signals comparable value

**vs Keyboard Maestro ($36 one-time):**
- **Us:** AI-powered, JITD, modern
- **Them:** Manual macros, setup required
- **Position:** "Next generation automation"
- **Pricing:** Higher ($240/year) justified by AI + JITD

**vs Claude Max ($20/month):**
- **Us:** Native app automation
- **Them:** File/browser automation
- **Position:** "Complementary, not competitive"
- **Pricing:** Same ($20) â†’ users can justify both

### 5. Growth Flexibility

**Can adjust down if needed:**
- Launch at $19.99
- If conversion low, offer 50% lifetime discount ($9.99/month)
- Or add "Basic" tier at $9.99 (limited features)
- Hard to raise price (anchoring), easy to lower

**Can add tiers:**
- Advanced tier at $29.99 (Tier 2: Accessibility APIs)

**Promotional strategies:**

## Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Price Too High (Low Conversion)

**Indicators:**
- Free users > 10K but conversions < 2%
- Trial users don't activate Pro features
- Feedback: "Too expensive for what it does"

**Mitigation:**
- Launch annual discount: $99/year (limited time)
- Add Basic tier: $9.99/month (fewer features)
- Offer payment plans: $299 lifetime in 3 payments
- Student/educator discounts
- Free Pro for public workflows (influencer strategy)

**Trigger:** If < 2% conversion after 1,000 free users

### Risk 2: Feature Parity (Why Pay?)

**Indicators:**
- Free tier (open source) good enough for most users
- Community builds UI wrapper for free
- Hard to articulate Pro value

**Mitigation:**
- Keep proprietary features genuinely better (AI workflow builder)
- Faster development cycle on Pro features
- Support and onboarding as key differentiator
- Enterprise features (team, management) can't be open sourced easily

**Trigger:** If conversion < 1% consistently

### Risk 3: Competition (Price War)

**Indicators:**
- Competitor launches similar product at $9.99/month
- Existing tools add JITD-like features
- Market commoditization

**Mitigation:**
- First-mover advantage (community, workflows)
- Multi-platform (Windows, Linux) = broader moat
- Non-scriptable app support (Tier 2/3) = differentiation
- Brand and community (can't be copied quickly)

**Trigger:** Direct competitor with JITD at lower price

### Risk 4: Market Too Small

**Indicators:**
- Can't get to 10,000 free users in 12 months
- Limited interest in native app automation
- AI agentic space cools off

**Mitigation:**
- Pivot to narrower market (developer tools only)
- Enterprise focus (higher price, fewer users needed)
- Windows launch (14x market)
- Partner with app developers (distribution channel)

**Trigger:** < 5,000 free users after 12 months
